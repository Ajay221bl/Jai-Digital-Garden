# inductive logic


inductive logic starts with specific observations or instances and then formulates a general conclusion _based on the evidence_. The conclusions might not be entirely accurate.
we try to find the _cause_ for a particular observation. 
- for example, ten flips of coin yielded 7 heads. _is it a fair coin or a biased one?_
- this type of question is related to [[Notes/inductive logic\|inductive reasoning]] 


> A phenomena is observed, we try to find the cause behind the phenomena by looking for the evidence? were the outcomes observed were due to A or due to B. if A then what is the evidence? if B then what is the evidence?

the conclusions drawn from inductive logic/reasoning gets revised from time to time as the new data gets available to us.
