# addition rule
> applicable where events are [[Notes/mutually exclusive sets or events\|mutually exclusive]] (cannot happen simultaneously)


addition rule states that the [[Notes/total probability\|total probability]] of one or another of the [[Notes/mutually exclusive sets or events\|mutually exclusive]] events occurring is the sum of their individual probabilities.. 

for example:
if you have the option to either flip a coin or roll a die, the outcome of these [[Notes/event\|events]] are [[Notes/mutually exclusive sets or events\|mutually exclusive]]  as we cannot do both of them at the same time.

if you flip a coin, the number of possible outcomes are 2.
if you roll a die, the number of possible outcomes are 6.

according to [[Notes/addition rule\|addition rule]], the total number of potential outcomes is $2+6=8$, assuming you choose to perform only one of these actions.
