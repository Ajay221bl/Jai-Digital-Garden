# normal [[Notes/probability distributions\|distribution]] (bell curve)
The normal distribution is a bell-shaped [[Notes/probability distributions\|probability distribution]] characterized by its symmetric shape centered around the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]

Many natural phenomena, such as height and blood pressure, are approximately normally distributed
![Pasted image 20250801212223.png](/img/user/Notes/Pasted%20image%2020250801212223.png)

#### important points to note from the above histograms
1. The distribution is symmetrical around its center, or [[Notes/mean\|mean]]. This means that the probability of finding a value greater than or lesser than mean would be 0.5
2. Due to its _symmetrical and unimodal nature_, the [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] takes on a bell-shaped curve, with a single peak at the mean. This shape indicates that values closer to the mean are more likely to be observed than values further away
3. The [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviation]] indicates the extent to which the distribution is thin or flat. The standard deviation can be estimated by the following formula:
	-  $$
\frac{0.399}{\text{maximum PDF associated with the normal distribution under observation}}
$$
	-  The $0.399$ represents the value of maximum [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] from a [[Notes/standard normal distribution\|standard normal distribution]]
4. The [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] achieves its maximum when the value for x is the [[Notes/mean\|mean]], regardless of the [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviation]].
