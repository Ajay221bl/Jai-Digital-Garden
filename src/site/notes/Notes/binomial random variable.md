# binomial random variable
the binomial random variable often denoted by $X$ represents the total count of successes in the $n$ number of trials. The possible values for $X$ are whole numbers ranging from $0$ (no successes at all) to $n$ (success in every trial).


for example, if you toss a fair coin 10 times and let $X$ be the number of times it lands on heads, then $X$ is a [[Notes/binomial random variable\|binomial random variable]]. In this case the $X$ can take any integer value from $0$ and $10$.
