# continuous random variables
a continuous [[Notes/random variable\|random variable]] can take on an uncountable number of values within a given range

for example: Height of a student
The random variable $H$ could be the height of a randomly chosen student. The value could be 165cm, 165.1cm, 165.11 cm


The range of possible values for a [[Notes/continuous random variables\|continuous random variable]] is typically denoted by an interval such as $[a,b]$, where $a$ and $b$ represent lower and upper bounds of the interval respectively. For instance the interval for the Boston Marathon is $[2,6]$ because the fastest runners in the world can't complete the course in less than 2 hours and 6 hours is the cutoff time.
Although every possible value between $2 \text{ and }6$ hours has some [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of occurrence, it doesn't necessarily mean they have equal probabilities of occurrence
