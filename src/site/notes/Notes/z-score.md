# z-score
z-score also known as [[Notes/z-score\|standard score]], is a statistical measure that indicates how many [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviations]] a particular value is from the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]. It is calculated using the following formula:
$$
z= \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}
$$
- $z$ is the z-score
- x is an individual data point.
- $\mu$ is the [[Notes/mean\|mean]] of the distribution
- $\sigma$ is the [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviation]] of this [[Notes/probability distributions\|distribution]]


- the _positive_ [[Notes/z-score\|z-score]] value indicates that the particular value is above the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]
- the _negative_ [[Notes/z-score\|z-score]] value indicates that the particular value is below the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]

-  the magnitude of the z-score reflects how many [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviations]] the value is from the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]. The larger magnitude means the point is farther from the [[Notes/mean\|mean]]

>[[Notes/z-score\|z-score standardization]] is generally preferred over [[Notes/min-max scaling\|min-max scaling normalization]] when your data has [[Notes/outliers\|outliers]] or when you're using [[Notes/algorithm\|algorithms]] that assume your data follows a [[Notes/normal distribution\|gaussian distribution]].
