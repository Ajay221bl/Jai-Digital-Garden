# empirical probability
this probability is derived from trials or real-world observations

$$
\text{Probability(Event)} = \frac{\text{number of successes observed}}{\text{number of observations made}}
$$

rainfall example:

$$
\text{Probability(rainfall)} = \frac{\text{number of rainy days}}{\text{total number of days}}
$$
$$
\text{Probability(rainfall)} = \frac{\text{{75}}}{\text{365}}
$$


>the 75 number is derived from the observation of continuous 75 days of rain over that span. This is why it is called _empirical probability_
