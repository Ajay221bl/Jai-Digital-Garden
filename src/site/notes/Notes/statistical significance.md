# statistical significance
statistical significance is a conclusion drawn from a statistical test that an observed result is _unlikely to have occurred by [[random chance\|random chance]]_ alone. It means there's likely a real effect or relationship between the variables you're studying.

In simple terms, when you find something that is [[Notes/statistical significance\|statistically significant]], you are saying it's probably not a fluke.

statistical significance is determined by comparing a [[Notes/p-value\|p-value]] to a predetermined threshold called the [[Notes/level of significance\|significance level]]


##### example:

Imagine you're testing whether a new fertilizer increase crop yield.

- [[Notes/null hypothesis\|null hypothesis]]($H_{0}$): The new fertilizer has no effect on crop yield
- [[alternative hypothesis\|alternative hypothesis]]($H_{a}$): The new fertilizer increases crop yield.

You run your experiment and find that the fields with the new fertilizer produced, on average $15\%$ more crops. You then perform a [[Notes/statistical tests\|statistical test]] and get a [[Notes/p-value\|p-value]] of $0.02\%$.

To check for [[Notes/statistical significance\|statistical significance]], you compare this [[Notes/p-value\|p-value]] to your chosen [[Notes/level of significance\|significance level]], let's say $\alpha= 0.05$.
