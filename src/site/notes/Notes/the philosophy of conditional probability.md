# the philosophy of [[Notes/conditional probability\|conditional probability]]

- understanding [[Notes/conditional probability\|conditional probabilities]] enables us to navigate [[Notes/uncertainty\|uncertainty]] more effectively, as it allows for nuanced decision-making based on available and relevant information.
- By recognizing how the [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of an [[Notes/event\|event]] changes with additional data points, we gain a more comprehensive understanding of the changing dynamics across various scenarios, from risk assessment to strategic planning.

##### examples to reinforce the concept of [[Notes/conditional probability\|conditional probability]]

__weather forecasting__:
- _without additional information_: The probability of rain tomorrow, based on historical record-keeping, might be $30\%$.
- _with additional information_(`dark clouds gathering, rainy conditions in neighboring states`): The probability of rain might increase to $70\%$

The [[Notes/sample space\|denominator]] or [[Notes/sample space\|sample space]], is reduced from all possible weather conditions to cloudy and rainy conditions, By reducing the denominator but keeping the numerator constant, the [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of rain increases.


__medical testing__:
- _without additional information_: The [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of having a noninfectious disease might be $5\%$, based on general popular statistics.
- _with additional information_(`positive test result`): The [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of having a noninfectious disease might increase to $80\%$.

In this case, the [[Notes/sample space\|sample space]] changes from the entire [[Notes/population\|population]] to only those individuals who test positive, significantly altering the [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]].

In the above mentioned examples, on one of the event depends on some another event or in other words, an event has an influence on the [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of another event
