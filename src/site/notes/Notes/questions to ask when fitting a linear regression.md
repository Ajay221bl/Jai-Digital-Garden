# questions to ask when fitting a [[Notes/linear regression\|linear regression]]

1. _Is there a meaningful relationship between [[Notes/explanatory variables\|independent variables]],  [[Notes/response variable\|dependent variable]]?_. Linear regression models the relationship between [[Notes/explanatory variables\|independent variables]] and [[Notes/response variable\|dependent variables]] under the assumption that the relationship is linear , allowing us to test for [[Notes/statistical significance\|statistical significance]].
2. _What is the nature of the relationship?_ Linear regression reveals if the relationship between variables is either positive or negative-- positive if they change in the same direction, negative if they change in the opposite direction.
3. _How strong is the relationship?_ Linear regression returns measures from which we can quantify the degree of association between variables, although these measures are most accurate when the [[Notes/explanatory variables\|independent variables]] are truly independent- a condition that is not always satisfied in real-world data
4. _Can we estimate changes in a [[Notes/response variable\|dependent variable]] from the [[Notes/variance\|variances]] in the [[Notes/explanatory variables\|independent variables]]?_ [[Notes/linear regression\|Linear regression]] provides coefficients that represent the estimated effect of each [[Notes/explanatory variables\|predictor]] on the [[Notes/response variable\|dependent variable]], allowing us to model expected outcomes under the assumption that the observed relationships are consistent.
5. _When fitting a [[Notes/multiple linear regression\|multiple linear regression]], where a [[Notes/response variable\|response variable]] is modeled against two or more [[Notes/explanatory variables\|predictors]], which [[Notes/explanatory variables\|predictors]] have a [[Notes/statistical significance\|statistically significant]] influence on changes in the [[Notes/response variable\|response variable]]?_ Linear regression helps identify which [[Notes/explanatory variables\|predictors]] are [[Notes/statistical significance\|statistically significant]], although this significance can be affected by factors like [[collinearity\|collinearity]] and other model assumptions.
6. _How well does the model fit the data?_ Linear regression estimates the goodness of fit, indicating how well the model explains and predicts variances in the [[Notes/response variable\|response variable]] under the assumption of linearity.
7. _Have model assumptions been met?_ [[model integrity\|model integrity]] (the internal soundness of the model) and [[Notes/model reliablity\|reliability]] (its consistency in returning valid results) are predicated on several assumptions being true. Tests and other checks are available to establish whether or not these assumptions have been satisfied
