# [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] of [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]]

The [[Notes/probability density function\|probability density function]] of the [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] is given by the following equation:
$$
\LARGE{f(x|\mu,\sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{ 2\pi }}\times e^{ -\frac{(x-\mu)^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}} }}
$$
- $f(x|\mu,\sigma)$ represents the [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] of a given value
- $x$ is the random variable
- $\mu$ is the [[Notes/mean\|mean]] or average, of the distribution
- $\sigma$ is the [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviation]], which measures the spread or [[Notes/dispersion\|dispersion]] of the [[Notes/probability distributions\|distribution]] .
- $e$ is the base of the [[natural logarithm\|natural logarithm]], approximately equal to $2.72$
- $\pi$ is the mathematical constant pi, approximately equals to $3.14$

> The PDF equation for the [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] would be different for [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] calculated for some other type of distribution. The above given probability density equation would apply exclusively in case of [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] only.



> The [[Notes/probability density function\|PDF]] of a [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] describes the relative likelihood of different outcomes, providing insights into the distribution's shape and spread without yielding the actual probabilities. To compute actual probabilities from a [[Notes/standard normal distribution\|standard normal distribution]], we must use the [[Notes/cumulative distribution function\|cumulative distribution function]]. this [[Notes/cumulative distribution function\|CDF]] can be found in the [[Notes/z-score table\|z-score table]].
