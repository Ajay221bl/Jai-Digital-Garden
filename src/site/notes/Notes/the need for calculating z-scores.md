# the need for calculating [[Notes/z-score\|z-scores]]



#### standardization for meaningful  comparisions
imagine we have two students who have taken different standardized tests. One student scores 650 on the SAT, and the other scores 28 on the ACT. How can we objectively determine who performed better relative to their peers? This is where z-scores become invaluable.

[[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distributions]] can have vastly different [[Notes/mean\|means]] and [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviations]]. A [[Notes/z-score\|z-score]] standardizes a raw data point by expressing it in terms of how many [[Notes/standard deviation\|standard deviations]] it is away from the [[Notes/mean\|mean]].


By converting the SAT and ACT scores to z-scores, we can place them on a common scale: [[Notes/standard normal distribution\|standard normal distribution]]. This would allow us for a direct and meaningful comparison of scores from different normal distributions.


#### effortless probability calculations
calculating [[Notes/z-score\|z-scores]] could help us skip the work of calculating the [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of a certain value occurring within a specific [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] using complex [[integral calculus\|integral calculus]].

by calculation the [[Notes/z-score\|z-scores]] we can easily utilize the [[Notes/z-score table\|z-score table]]  to find the probability of a certain value.
