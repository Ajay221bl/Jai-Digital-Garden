# discrete uniform distribution

the discrete [[Notes/uniform distribution\|uniform distribution]] is a [[Notes/probability distributions\|probability distribution]] with a constant [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]]; that is, every value, or every [[Notes/discrete random variables\|discrete random variable]] , within a predefined range has an equal probability of occurring.

for example, rolling a fair six-sided die (the probability of rolling any number from 1 to 6 is $\frac{1}{6}$)

the discrete uniform distribution is sometimes called [[Notes/discrete uniform distribution\|rectangular distribution]] because when plotted, the distribution takes on a rectangular shape. 


it is typically denoted as $X\sim U(a,b)$, where a and b represent, respectively, the lower and upper bounds of possible values.

__Conditions for considering a [[Notes/probability distributions\|probability distribution]] as [[Notes/discrete uniform distribution\|discrete uniform distribution]]:__

1. each outcome within the range $[a.b]$ must have the same [[Notes/probability\|Notes/probability]] of occurrence.
2. the [[Notes/probability distributions\|distribution]] only applies to a finite set of values within the specified range
3. The sum of all probabilities within the range must therefore equal 1
4. each outcome is independent of the others.
