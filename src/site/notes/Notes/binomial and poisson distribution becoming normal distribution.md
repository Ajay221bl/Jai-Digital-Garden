# [[Notes/binomial distribution\|binomial]] & [[Notes/poisson distribution\|poisson]] distribution becoming [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]]

a [[Notes/binomial distribution\|binomial distribution]]  becomes like a [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] when number of trials $n$ is sufficiently large. This allows for the use of [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] to approximate the [[Notes/binomial distribution\|binomial distribution]], which can simplify calculations significantly.
##### key factors of approximation:
1. large sample size ($n$).
2. probability of success ($p$) is close to $0.5$




similarly, a [[Notes/poisson distribution\|poisson distribution]] can be accurately approximated by a [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] when its [[Notes/mean\|mean]] and [[Notes/variance\|variance]] represented by $\lambda$ are large.
##### rule of thumb
The approximation is generally considered good when $\lambda\geq{10}$  


---
the reason why [[Notes/binomial distribution\|binomial]] and [[Notes/poisson distribution\|poisson]] distributions trend towards a [[Notes/normal distribution\|normal distribution]] is explained by the [[Notes/central limit theorem\|central limit theorem]].
